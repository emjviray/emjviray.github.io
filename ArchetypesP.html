Project Description:

This project analyzes how environmental and structural characteristics of all 30 MLB stadiums influence pitcher performance across five defined archetypes: Power, Finesse, Groundball, Balanced, and Veteran. Using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality of stadium features (e.g., altitude, temperature, wind, and field dimensions) and K-Means clustering to group ballparks by similarity, the study quantifies how different pitching styles align with specific stadium conditions. By combining Statcast-derived pitching metrics with ballpark factors, the model identifies optimal pitcher–stadium pairings and reveals how external environments impact outcomes such as contact quality and run prevention. The results provide data-driven insights for player evaluation, game strategy, and roster construction, bridging sports analytics with applied machine learning.
