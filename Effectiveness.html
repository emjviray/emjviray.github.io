Project Description:

UCSD Tritions Baseball Head Coach Eric Newman came to me for problem that he had with his pitchers. Nic Gregson and Jake Villar experienced issues when being kept in the game for an extended period of time. He wanted an Exploratory Data Analysis of their batters faced data to determine when we should pull them form the game so that we prepare our bullpen before thingd got out of hand on the mound for these two players So I built a pitch-by-pitch analytics pipeline for UCSD pitchers Nic Gregson and Jake Villar using live Trackman data queried directly from Google BigQuery. The project tracks how each pitcher's effectiveness evolves across a game by analyzing performance in rolling windows of batters faced (every 3), pitch count (every 10), time through the order, and inning. Advanced baseball metrics including OPS against, wOBA, xwOBA, Hard Hit%, BB%, Zone%, and velocity trends were computed from raw pitch-level data and aggregated to the plate appearance level. Fatigue and command indicators were visualized through annotated line charts, revealing how pitch velocity, zone control, and contact quality shift as each outing progresses. The full season analysis was also compared against each pitcher's last three appearances to capture recent performance trends, providing the coaching staff with actionable, data-driven insight into pitcher workload and in-game effectiveness.
